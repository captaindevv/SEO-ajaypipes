### Why only 11 of 217

`Article and blogs - Sheet1.csv` contains 217 rows. Sorting them out:

- **23 rows** are genuine, individually-named newspapers/wire services (Business Standard, Times of India, Dainik Bhaskar, Tribune, ANI, regional-language mastheads, etc.).
- Of those 23, only **11 have a populated `Publish URL`** — the rest are still "On Process" / untranslated per the sheet and have no live URL to cite yet.
- **The remaining ~194 rows** (rows named things like "India State News," "Rajasthan Headlines," "Nagaland News 24x7," "Mizoram News Buzz") are a **PR-wire syndication network**: every single one republishes the identical ANI press release ("India's plumbing industry faces urgent need for standards and consumer awareness") on a near-identical `.co.in`/`.in` domain with a generic, interchangeable name. This is a paid press-release distribution pattern, not editorial coverage.

**Do not link to the syndication-network rows.** Citing 190 near-duplicate low-authority domains publishing the same press release is a link-scheme signal, not an authority signal — it would actively hurt E-E-A-T rather than help it. Only the 11 rows below are approved.

### Framing rule for press citations

These are third-party citations that validate an industry-context claim (standards, consumer awareness, homeowner survey findings, engineer warnings) — cite them as independent corroboration, not as if Ajay authored them. Never imply the article is an Ajay press release when linking from blog body copy; standard framing is "as [Publication] reported" or "a [Publication] survey found," with the link on the publication name or the specific finding, never on a generic "read more."

### Standards referenced but not directly link-worthy

IS 4985 (UPVC pipes) has no confirmed live `.gov.in` PDF URL as of this research pass — only third-party mirrors (`law.resource.org`, etc.) were found, and third-party mirrors are not "government" sources. If a post needs to cite IS 4985 specifically, link to the BIS main site (`bis.gov.in`) and name the standard in text ("IS 4985") rather than link a non-government mirror. Re-verify before adding a direct IS 4985 link.

### Rule for this bucket

Match the government source to the specific claim. Don't drop the BIS homepage into every post reflexively — a Deepline/borewell post pairs naturally with Jal Jeevan Mission or CPHEEO, a CPVC hot-water post pairs with the BIS CPVC product manual or NSF, an ISI-certification claim pairs with the BIS standard-mark verification page. If no government source genuinely fits the post's claims, don't force one in.
